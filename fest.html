Festivals

Janmashtami:

Celebrates Krishna's birth, observed with fasting, prayers, and reenactments of his childhood.

Govardhan Puja:

Commemorates Krishna lifting the Govardhan Hill to protect villagers from torrential rains.

Holi:

The festival of colors, associated with Krishna's playful interactions with the gopis in Vrindavan.

Radhastami:

Celebrates the birth of Radha, Krishna's divine consort.

Gopashtami:

Marks Krishna's transition from a cowherd boy to protector of cows (Gopala).

Yamuna Jayanti:

Celebrates the birth of the Yamuna River, which is closely associated with Krishna's childhood.

Each of these festivals involves devotional singing, prayers, and sometimes reenactments of Krishna's divine pastimes.
